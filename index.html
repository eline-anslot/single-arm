Proposed method

Step 1: Model fitting:

h [ E ( Y | 𝐗 , S ) ] = β 0 + 𝛃 𝟏 𝐗 + β 2 S

Step 2: Predicting:

Y ^ = h − 1 [ β 0 ^ + 𝛃 𝟏 ^ 𝐗 + β ^ 2 S ]

Proposed method

Step 3: Model fitting:

h [ E ( Y ^ | 𝐗 ) ] = γ 0 + 𝛄 𝟏 𝐗

Step 4: Predicting:

Y ^ ′ = h − 1 [ γ 0 ^ + 𝛄 𝟏 ^ 𝐗 ]

Proposed method

Decision at interim: Z-test with critical value based on the \(PET(\mu_0)\)
    • No additional assumptions
    • Adjusting for different short-term endpoints and baseline covariates
    • Asymptotically unbiased even with misspecified models
    • Asymptotically efficient when models are correct

    • Proportion of additional participants in the pipeline
        • But not everybody should be recruited at interim
        • Mukherjee et al. (2022) recommend that the duration to obtain the primary endpoint does not exceed 10% of the total estimated recruitment time

Discussion

    • Type I error rate inflated in small sample sizes
        • Estimator's variance leans on asymptotic theory
        • Decision at interim relies on an approximation of the standard normal distribution
